About

I'm a Master's student at The University of California, Berkeley studying Information Management and Systems (MIMS) with a concentration in natural language processing. My research relies on applying machine learning methods to problems involving large amounts of textual data.

I returned to school after working as a Back-End Software Engineer at Brightspot for 2 years. I architected the data pipelines and content solutions for clients like Mattress Firm. I gained robust experience migrating data, working with REST APIs and collaborating with client stakeholders using Java and MySQL.

In undergrad, I double-majored in Computer Science and English at The University of Virginia and graduated with High Honors. My thesis project, A Digital Analysis of the Lyrical Novel, was advised by Brad Pasanek. Other involvements at UVA include earning a Research Assistant position at The Institute for Advanced Technology in the Humanities (IATH) and assisting with Brad's Puzzle Poetry project.

The Projects section contains academic endeavors like my forthcoming analysis of details in fiction using language modeling and machine learning as well as personal areas of interest like data automation with Python and R. Check out the Blog for informal updates and experiments.

I'm on the job market! I'm interested in ML and NLP engineering summer internship roles. Please reach out if you think I'd be a good fit via email or LinkedIn!
