About

I am a Graduate student at The University of California, Berkeley pursuing a Master of Information and Management Science (MIMS) with a concentration in natural language processing. I'm centrally interested in the effect of technology on how we use and understand language.

I am returning to school after working as a Back-End Software Engineer at Brightspot for 2 years. I architected the data pipelines and content solutions for clients like Mattress Firm. I gained robust experience migrating data, working with REST APIs and collaborating with client stakeholders using Java and MySQL.

In undergrad, I studied Computer Science and English at The University of Virginia and graduated with High Honors. My thesis project, A Digital Analysis of the Lyrical Novel, was advised by Brad Pasanek. Other involvements at UVA include earning a Research Assistant position at UVA's Institute for Advanced Technology in the Humanities (IATH) and membership with Brad's wonderful Puzzle Poetry group.

The Projects section contains personal areas of interest like data automation and language modeling with Python and R. Check out the Blog for informal updates and experiments.

I'm on the job market! I am interested in ML and NLP engineering summer internship roles. Please reach out if you think I'd be a good fit via email or LinkedIn!
