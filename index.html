About

I'm a Master's student at UC Berkeley studying Information Management and Systems (MIMS) with a concentration in Data Science. I'm passionate about applying machine learning methods to solve problems involving large amounts of textual data.

I returned to school after working as a Back-End Software Engineer at Brightspot for 2 years. I architected the data pipelines and content solutions for clients like Mattress Firm and Janes. I gained robust experience modeling data, integrating and writing APIs, and collaborating across different teams with client stakeholders.

In undergrad, I double-majored in Computer Science and English at The University of Virginia and graduated with High Honors. My thesis project, A Digital Analysis of the Lyrical Novel, was advised by Brad Pasanek. Other involvements at UVA include earning a Research Assistant position at The Institute for Advanced Technology in the Humanities (IATH) and assisting with Brad's Puzzle Poetry project.

Academic projects like my ongoing, quantitative analysis of details in the novel, plus other personal projects like a language model trained on Supreme Court data, can be found in the Projects section. Check out the Blog for informal updates and experiments.

I'm on the job market! I'm interested in Data Science and NLP summer internship roles. Please reach out if you think I'd be a good fit via email or LinkedIn!
