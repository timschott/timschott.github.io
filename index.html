I am a Senior Data Scientist at Red Ventures, where I work on the Red Platform team building machine learning systems used across the organization. My work supports customer segmentation, funnel optimization, and large-scale decisioning, including contributions to a multi-armed bandit–based decision API.

I received a Master’s degree in Information Management and Systems from the University of California, Berkeley, with a focus on data science and natural language processing. My thesis examined multilingual factual knowledge recall in large language models; this work was published at EMNLP 2023. While at Berkeley, I served as a teaching assistant for Professor David Bamman’s natural language processing courses.

Prior to graduate school, I worked as a backend software engineer at Brightspot, where I designed data pipelines and content platforms for enterprise clients including Mattress Firm and Janes. This role emphasized data modeling, API design, and cross-functional collaboration.

I earned a BA in Computer Science and a BA in English from the University of Virginia, graduating with High Honors in 2019. My Distinguished Majors thesis was completed under the supervision of Professor Brad Pasanek, and I previously served as a research assistant at the Institute for Advanced Technology in the Humanities.
